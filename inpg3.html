Matching Eyeshadow to Eyecolor

Published: Septmeber 21st, 2017

This look brings out your eyes in a less daunting way. By highlighting and defining without creating an intimidating look. It causes a more natural definition to your eyes while still causing them to pop. By empowering you to be your best self it stays true to who you naturally are. Try this style for a more casual outcome while staying in the same routine as the nighttime steps. The best part is that it is very easy to convert to the nighttime look once you are ready to go out.

Blue Eyes

  • Lavender
  • Lilac
  • Copper

Green Eyes

Green eyes should turn to lighter blues and darker greens to fully accentuate their eye color. At night these colors will make lighter eyes stand out in the dimmer lighting. When doing a daytime smokey eye stick to neutrals that have a redder undertone. Some examples:

  • Emerald
  • Dark Olive Green
  • Forest Green
  • Merlot

Brown Eyes

Those with brown eyes should stick to colors that have lighter hues the power the lightness can give can brighten up your dark pupil. By taking gold it makes your eye look as if it is shining and brings out the natural browns of your eye. Some examples:

  • Grey
  • Gold
